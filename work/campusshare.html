CampusShare

A peer-to-peer rental and resale marketplace built exclusively for college students, segmented by institution through secure domain-based authentication.

Challenge

Students had no trusted, campus-specific way to exchange goods.

General marketplaces like Facebook and Craigslist lack institutional trust — buyers and sellers have no way to verify they're dealing with a fellow student. This creates friction, safety concerns, and low conversion for campus-specific transactions like textbook resale, furniture rental, and equipment lending.

The goal was a zero-friction platform where students could list and discover items knowing every participant shared their campus affiliation.

Approach

Institutional segmentation through domain authentication.

The core architecture decision was to gate access by verified university email domain. Users authenticate with their .edu address, which automatically segments their listings and feed into their institution's marketplace. No cross-campus noise — just your campus.

The UI was designed to keep listing creation under 60 seconds: minimal required fields, image upload, category selection, and a price. The browse experience mirrors the simplicity — grid-based, filterable by category, with a clean card layout.

Outcome

A live, functional marketplace serving real students.

CampusShare launched and is live at campusshareapp.com. The platform demonstrates a full MVP: user authentication, listing creation, browse and discovery, and institution-based segmentation — all built and designed from scratch as the sole founder.
